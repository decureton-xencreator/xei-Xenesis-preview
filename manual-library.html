XBM-103 · Approved operating content

Trust Before Technique

Listen. Do not interrupt. Remain calm. Do not argue. Do not make assumptions. Never overpromise. Be honest when an answer must be verified.

XBM-103 · Seven-Step Objection Framework

How a representative handles uncertainty

1

Listen

Let the customer finish. Do not prepare your answer while they are still speaking.

2

Acknowledge

Show the customer that the concern was heard without agreeing to an inaccurate assumption.

3

Clarify

Ask one natural question to understand what the concern actually means.

4

Educate

Provide the smallest useful amount of accurate information that reduces uncertainty.

5

Transition

Connect the answer to the next useful step in the customer journey.

6

Confirm

Check that the response addressed the real concern.

7

Close

Ask for the appropriate next commitment without pressure or overpromising.

Living Document Method

One decision can update the family

A statement about agent behavior belongs in XBM-101. A management or coaching principle belongs in XBM-102. An objection response belongs in XBM-103. A call phrase, discovery question or follow-up language belongs in XBM-104. Xen identifies the correct destination, proposes the change in context, and synchronizes approved inheritance across related publications.
